Overview

Robot interaction with humans is inevitable: autonomous cars navigate through crowded cities, assistive robots help end-users with daily living tasks, and human engineers iteratively tune robot objective functions. In this graduate seminar class, we will build the mathematical foundations for modeling human-robot interaction, investigate algorithms for robot learning from human data, and develop the tools to analyze the safety and reliability of robots deployed around people. The approaches covered will draw upon a variety of tools such as optimal control, dynamic game theory, Bayesian inference, and modern machine learning.
